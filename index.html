Consumer Protection

Consumer protection is the practice of safeguarding buyers of goods and services,
and the public, against unfair practices in the marketplace. Consumer protection
measures are often established by law. Such laws are intended to prevent
businesses from engaging in fraud or specified unfair practices in order
to gain an advantage over competitors or to mislead consumers.
They may also provide additional protection for the general public which may
be impacted by a product (or its production) even when they are not the direct
purchaser or consumer of that product. For example, government regulations may require
businesses to disclose detailed information about their productsâ€”particularly
in areas where public health or safety is an issue, such as with food or automobiles.
